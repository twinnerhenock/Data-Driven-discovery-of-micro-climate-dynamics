This project uses ERA5 reanalysis data for New York City, extracted over a spatial grid of 45 points covering the region. 
The dataset includes near-surface temperature, surface solar radiation, and wind speed, sampled hourly. 
Data was downloaded for two distinct periods: June–August (used for model training and equation discovery) and 
September (used exclusively for out-of-sample validation). The selected domain exhibits mixed microclimate regimes, 
including coastal and inland dynamics, which introduces variability in temperature behavior and challenges for modeling. 
This heterogeneity is particularly relevant for evaluating both global and clustered modeling approaches such as SINDy and CSINDy.
